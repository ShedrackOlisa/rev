Introduction

The iPhone 15 Pro Max represents Apple's most ambitious smartphone yet, packing cutting-edge technology into a refined titanium design. As the flagship of the iPhone 15 lineup, it brings significant upgrades including the new A17 Pro chip, an advanced camera system with tetraprism zoom, and the innovative Action button.

After two weeks of extensive testing, we've evaluated every aspect of this device to determine whether it's worth its premium price tag and if it truly sets a new standard for smartphones in 2023.

Design and Build Quality

Apple has finally moved away from stainless steel to a brushed titanium frame, resulting in a device that's both more premium and surprisingly lighter (221g vs. 240g in the 14 Pro Max). The matte textured back glass provides excellent grip, and the thinner bezels make the display appear even more immersive.

The new Action button replaces the mute switch, offering customizable functionality. While it takes some getting used to, we found it particularly useful for quickly launching the camera or flashlight. The USB-C port is a welcome change, though data transfer speeds vary based on the cable used.

Display

The 6.7-inch Super Retina XDR display remains industry-leading, with several notable improvements:

  • Brighter 2000 nits peak HDR brightness (up from 1600 nits)
  • More power-efficient LTPO OLED technology
  • Thinner bezels (1.55mm vs. 2.15mm)
  • Improved outdoor visibility with new anti-reflective coating

We measured color accuracy at an impressive ΔE < 1.5, making this one of the most color-accurate displays available. The always-on display is more customizable and less distracting than last year's implementation.

Performance

The new A17 Pro chip built on 3nm process is an absolute powerhouse:

Pros

  • 20% faster CPU performance than A16
  • 40% more powerful GPU with hardware ray tracing
  • Excellent thermal management
  • Can run console-quality games like Resident Evil 4
  • 16-core Neural Engine for advanced machine learning

Cons

  • Overkill for most everyday tasks
  • Some throttling under sustained heavy loads
  • Limited by iOS in terms of pro applications

In our benchmarks, the iPhone 15 Pro Max scored 2900 in single-core and 7200 in multi-core Geekbench 6 tests, making it the fastest smartphone chip currently available. The 8GB of RAM helps with multitasking, though Android flagships now offer up to 16GB.

Camera System

The camera upgrades are where the 15 Pro Max truly shines:

The new 48MP main sensor captures stunning detail, especially in good lighting. The 5x tetraprism zoom (120mm equivalent) is a game-changer for distant subjects, though it requires decent lighting. Night mode performance has improved by about 1.5 stops compared to the 14 Pro Max.

Video capabilities remain unmatched, with Cinematic mode now supporting 4K/30fps and the new Log encoding giving professionals more flexibility in post-production.

Battery Life

Despite the more powerful chip, battery life sees modest improvements:

  • 12 hours 45 mins screen-on time (mixed use)
  • 18 hours video playback
  • Excellent standby efficiency
  • Faster charging (27W wired, 15W MagSafe)

While not class-leading, the battery performance is respectable given the power of the A17 Pro. The USB-C port enables faster charging (50% in 30 mins with compatible charger), though Apple still doesn't include a charger in the box.

Verdict

4.8 / 5.0

The iPhone 15 Pro Max is the most capable iPhone ever made and arguably the best smartphone available today. While the upgrades over the 14 Pro Max are evolutionary rather than revolutionary, the combination of titanium design, phenomenal camera system, and unmatched performance makes this an easy recommendation for those wanting the absolute best.

That said, the $1,199 starting price is hard to justify for most users, especially when the standard iPhone 15 offers many of the same core experiences at a much lower cost. This is a device for power users, photography enthusiasts, and those who demand the latest technology regardless of price.
